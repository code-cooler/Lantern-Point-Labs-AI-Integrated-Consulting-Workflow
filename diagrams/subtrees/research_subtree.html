Research & Problem Definition

This subtree details the steps involved in collecting data and defining the problem to be solved.

Phase Overview

The Research & Problem Definition phase is where data is collected to understand the client's situation and define the problem to be solved. This phase involves:

  • Data Collection: Gathering relevant data from multiple sources
  • Stakeholder Interviews: Conducting interviews with key stakeholders
  • Market Research: Researching relevant market trends and factors
  • Problem Definition Workshop: Session to clearly define the problem to be solved
  • Consolidate Research Findings: Bringing together all research into a cohesive format

The outcome of this phase is a well-defined problem statement and sufficient data to begin analysis.

AI Research Enhancement

Purpose: Identify gaps in collected data and synthesize initial findings to guide further research.

When to use:

  • After initial stakeholder interviews and data collection
  • Before finalizing the research phase
  • When dealing with large volumes of qualitative data

Key Capabilities:

  • Research Gap Analysis: Identifying missing data points or perspectives
  • Initial Data Synthesis: Finding patterns across multiple data sources
  • Theme Extraction: Identifying consistent themes across stakeholder input

Example Prompt:

"Review these stakeholder interviews and identify: 1) consistent themes, 2) potential contradictions, 3) topics mentioned by only one stakeholder, and 4) important questions that remain unanswered."

Expected Output: The AI will analyze qualitative data to identify patterns, contradictions, and gaps, helping to focus subsequent research efforts.
